The JHU open source programs office (OSPO is running a Free and Open Source (FOSS) Contributor Fund that is described below. Through this fund, you will have the opportunity to vote for open source projects to receive $10,000 and nominate projects or software as well.

The FOSS Contributor Fund program has been established to build a community of interest that will provide financial support for open source projects through a democratic and open process. The JHU OSPO has received a grant from the Sloan Foundation for the first year of our program through which we will hold votes that will rdesult in $10,000 being awarded to an open source project based on voting from within JHU. More information about the FOSS Contributor Fund program is available at:

https://engineering.indeedblog.com/blog/2019/11/foss-fund-adopters

The voting page and nomination page are linked on the left side menu. You can access the voting page directly at https://foss-fund.ospo.library.jhu.edu/vote

You also have a chance to nominate open source projects for subsequent rounds of monthly voting. You can access the nomination form at:
https://foss-fund.ospo.library.jhu.edu/nominate

If you have any questions, please feel free to contact us at ospo@jhu.edu.
